Diamond cut refers to the proportions, symmetry, and polish of a stone's facets, which determine how well it interacts with light to produce brightness, fire, and sparkle. It is distinct from the diamond's shape, which is its outward physical appearance (e.g., round, oval, square).

The quality of a diamond's cut is widely considered the most important of the 4Cs (Cut, Color, Clarity, and Carat) because it has the greatest impact on the diamond's visual beauty and brilliance.

A "diamond cut set" usually means a jewelry set featuring diamonds, but the term "diamond cut" itself refers to a diamond's proportions, symmetry, and polish that dictate its brilliance, with common shapes being Round Brilliant, Princess, and Emerald. For a set, you'd look at the specific cuts (like oval brilliant, round brilliant) and details like clarity (VS2), color (Fancy Light Yellow), carat weight, and the setting type (prong, halo) to understand its quality and style, alongside the main gemstones like pearls.
